## Pronunciation & Name
**Arétier** (Ah-ray-tee-ay)

### Etymology
The name fuses two roots:
- **Arete (ἀρετή)** — Ancient Greek. The concept of supreme excellence, the fullest expression of human potential, moral virtue, and valour in the face of resistance. Not perfection — *earned* excellence.
- **-ier** — French artisan suffix. A *couturier* makes couture. A *chapelier* makes hats. An *Arétier* makes excellence itself.

**Literal translation:** *"The Maker of Excellence."*

---

## Brand Philosophy

Arétier does not make sneakers. It makes instruments of human will.

Every silhouette we release carries the weight of a single belief: that a person is capable of more than they currently are, and that what they wear can be a daily reminder of that gap — and the obligation to close it. We build for the person who is becoming, not the person who has arrived.

We do not chase trends. We study them, absorb them, and then answer them on our own terms.

---

## Core Brand Pillars

### 1. Valour
Not the absence of doubt, but motion despite it. Arétier products are designed for the person who shows up even when the conditions are wrong. Our materials are tested for durability under stress — because our customer is tested for the same.

### 2. Strength
Structural integrity in every layer. We do not add ornament where function should live. Strength is expressed through restraint: clean lines, engineered support, materials chosen for performance first. A shoe that looks strong *is* strong.

### 3. Persistence
The Arétier customer is not defined by their peaks. They are defined by their consistency. Our drops are rare because we believe in doing one thing with total commitment, not many things with diluted attention.

## Voice & Tone

### Always:
- **Direct.** Short sentences. We trust the customer to complete the thought.
- **Earned.** Never hype for its own sake. Every claim must be supportable.
- **Grounded in craft.** Reference the material, the construction, the engineering decision — not the feeling we hope it creates.
- **Quiet confidence.** We do not shout. We state.

### Never:
- Exclamation marks in product copy (acceptable in social, sparingly)
- Corporate wellness language ("empower," "journey," "transform")
- Trend-chasing adjectives ("fire," "heat," "drip")
- Passive voice in product descriptions
- Starting a social post with "Introducing," "Excited to," or "We are pleased"
- Price mentions in social copy

---

## Vocabulary — Use These
- *Forged* (not made)
- *Built* (not crafted, unless context is about handwork)
- *Ridge, trail, field, arena* (terrain as metaphor)
- *Silhouette* (not "shoe" or "sneaker" in premium copy)
- *Carry* (as in "this silhouette carries...")
- *The Arétier customer* (refer to your audience this way in internal docs)
- *Drop* (not "release" or "launch")

## Vocabulary — Avoid These
- "Luxury" (implied, never stated)
- "Premium" (same)
- "For the culture"
- "Limited edition" (all drops are limited; stating it is redundant)
- "Game-changer," "next-level," "elevate"

## Aesthetic DNA

**Color palette tendency:** Earth architecture — sage, slate, raw umber, bone, carbon. Accents from nature: burnt sienna, glacier blue, oxidized copper. Never neon unless it is the sole accent and it earns its presence.

**Silhouette tendency:** Trail runners, high-top field boots, low-profile trainers. Anti-chunky. Anti-maximalist. The shoe should look like it belongs on terrain and on concrete simultaneously.

**Material tendency:** Technical fabrics (ripstop, Cordura), natural textures (suede, full-grain), recycled composites where structurally appropriate. No vinyl, no synthetic patent leather.

---

## Product Description Formula

Structure every product description as follows:
1. **Provenance line** (1 sentence) — Where does this design come from? What idea, terrain, or resistance does it answer?
2. **Construction statement** (2-3 sentences) — What is actually built here? Materials, structure, key engineering decision.
3. **The customer truth** (1-2 sentences) — Who is this for, without naming them? What does wearing this say about their commitment?

**Target length:** 120–180 words. Never under 100, never over 200.

---

## Social Copy Formula

**Tweet structure options:**
- `[Terrain/context line.] [Product name] [Drop date/time].` — sparse, confident
- `[A truth about the customer.] [Product name] →` — philosophy-first
- `[Drop alert 🚨] [Product name] [hits / arrives / drops] [Day Time].` — functional, no fluff

**Image caption structure:**
- 1-2 lines maximum
- No hashtags in the caption body (move to first comment or end)
- Always end with a period or arrow →, never an exclamation mark

## Competitive Context

Arétier is aware of, but not defined by:
- **Nike ACG** — functional trail aesthetic, but corporate. We are independent.
- **Salehe Bembury** collaborations — proof that trail and premium intersect profitably.
- **New Balance 2002R / 990** — heritage and restraint done well. We respect the playbook.
- **Satisfy Running** — the closest philosophical cousin. They own performance poetry; we own performance philosophy.

---

## What Arétier Is Not

Not streetwear. Not athleisure. Not a collab-dependent brand. Not ironic. Not nostalgic.

Arétier is a forward-facing commitment to the idea that what you wear while becoming yourself matters — and should be built accordingly.
